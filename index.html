About Me

Welcome to my corner of the internet! I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Mayur Naik, and a GenAI PhD Intern at Oracle's OCI. My research focuses on Neurosymbolic Programming and Synthesis, with the goal of enabling foundation models to reason in a reliable and trustworthy manner. My work integrates symbolic reasoning directly into ML architectures by developing frameworks for scalable neurosymbolic training (Dolphin), efficient inference-time reasoning (TorchQL, SQRL), and foundational techniques for program analysis, synthesis, and verification (Code2Inv, CodeTrek, Sporq).

I am also actively interested in related applications of this work. This includes exploring Logic-Informed Reinforcement Learning to solve sparse-reward problems in LLM post-training (building on Code2Inv), as well as AI for Science, where program synthesis can integrate domain knowledge (like physics or medicine) to improve model trustworthiness and enable new discoveries.
