Research Interests

üß† Scaling Neurosymbolic Learning

Integrating reasoning as a core component of machine learning architectures. My research develops scalable techniques for complex tasks and modern datasets, including programmable frameworks for neurosymbolic training (Dolphin) and efficient symbolic reasoning during inference (TorchQL, SQRL).

üíª ML for Program Analysis

Enhancing ML models' capabilities to reason over programs and synthesize code. This encompasses flexible relational representations for source code (CodeTrek), interactive code exploration environments (Sporq), and deep learning frameworks for program verification (Code2Inv).

üéØ Logic-Informed RL

Addressing the challenge of sparse rewards in teaching LLMs complex reasoning. My research explores using symbolic programs to provide effective partial rewards for guiding policy learning, as demonstrated in Code2Inv. I am actively expanding this work to develop logic-informed reward systems for RL-based post-training of large language models.

üî¨ AI for Science

Applying neurosymbolic methods to scientific discovery by integrating logic and symbolic reasoning with neural networks. This includes developing methods to incorporate domain knowledge‚Äîphysics, medical information‚Äîinto LLMs for improved trustworthiness in data-sparse scientific fields. My work on scalable logical verification (Dolphin) and program synthesis provides a strong foundation for this direction.

If you're interested in working with me or discussing any of these research areas, please feel free to reach out via email at asnaik@seas.upenn.edu. I'm always excited to explore potential collaborations and new research directions.
